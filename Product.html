Type Of Wood

  • Softwood
    • Sengon / Albasia / Falcata

      • is one of the pioneer multipurpose tree species in Indonesia. This tree is an excellent material for industry because of its good growth speed, can live in various soil conditions, and is a good raw material for the wood panel and plywood industries. Sengon trees are very important in agroforestry farming systems in some parts of Indonesia. Sengon wood properties include durability class IV/V and class IV-V with a specific gravity of 0.33 (0.24-0.49). The wood is soft and has shrinkage values in the radial and tangential directions are 2.5 percent and 5.2 percent (wet to kiln dry).

  • Hardwood
    • Meranti

      • is a well-known tropical plant species. Meranti trees can grow in lowlands and rainforests such as Sumatra, Kalimantan, Maluku and Sulawesi. Red meranti is classified as a light to medium-weight hardwood. Its specific gravity ranges from 0.3-0.86 at 15% water content. The heartwood is pale pink, brownish pink, to dark red or even dark brownish red in color. According to their strength, red meranti species can be classified in strength class II-IV; while their durability is classified in class III-IV.

    • Jabon

      • is one type of wood or tree that grows very fast and can thrive in tropical forests. The diameter of the trunk can grow around 10cm / year, the height of the trunk at the age of 12 years can reach 20 meters, so that at the age of 6-8 years it can be harvested. Jabon wood is slightly yellowish white without visible fibers, so jabon wood is needed in the plywood industry, raw materials for furniture and furniture, and non-construction building materials. This advantage makes marketing jabon wood not at all difficult, even the plywood industry is ready to buy at any time in large quantities.
